# A0 Foundation — Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1: Confusing "sie" vs "Sie"

### ❌ Wrong
"Sie ist krank." (ambiguous — could be she or you formal)

### ✅ Correct
- "sie ist krank." — **She** is sick (lowercase in writing)
- "Sie sind krank." — **You** are sick (formal, always capitalized)

**Tip**: Context and capitalization matter!

---

## Mistake 2: Wrong Sein Conjugation

### ❌ Wrong
- "Ich are..." (English form)
- "Du ist krank" (wrong form)
- "Wir bin glücklich" (wrong form)

### ✅ Correct
- "Ich **bin** glücklich" — I am happy
- "Du **bist** krank" — You are sick
- "Wir **sind** glücklich" — We are happy

---

## Mistake 3: Wrong Haben Conjugation

### ❌ Wrong
- "Ich have..." (English)
- "Er haben Hunger" (wrong form)
- "Wir has Durst" (English, not German)

### ✅ Correct
- "Ich **habe** Hunger" — I am hungry
- "Er **hat** Hunger" — He is hungry
- "Wir **haben** Durst" — We are thirsty

---

## Mistake 4: Forgetting Verb-First in Questions

### ❌ Wrong
"Du bist intelligent?" (sounds like a statement)

### ✅ Correct
"**Bist** du intelligent?" (proper question)

**Rule**: Verb comes FIRST in German questions!

---

## Mistake 5: Mixing Sein and Haben for Feelings

### ❌ Wrong
- "Ich habe glücklich" (I have happy?)
- "Du hast müde" (You have tired?)

### ✅ Correct
- "Ich **bin** glücklich" — I am happy
- "Du **bist** müde" — You are tired
- But: "Ich **habe** Hunger" — I have hunger (correct in German!)

**Rule**: Adjectives use "sein", but states like hunger/thirst use "haben"

---

## Mistake 6: Missing Articles with Haben

### ❌ Wrong
- "Ich habe Bruder" (missing article)
- "Du hast Hund" (missing article)

### ✅ Correct
- "Ich habe **einen** Bruder" — I have a brother
- "Du hast **einen** Hund" — You have a dog

2. Du **hast** Hunger?
3. Wir **sind** glücklich.
4. **Bist** du intelligent?
